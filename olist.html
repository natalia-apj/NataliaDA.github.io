Business Data Analytics - Brazil

PROJECT DESCRIPTION:
The project was focused on analysing Brazilian Olist Store public dataset containing information of 100 000 orders from 2016 to 2018. Olist Store functions as marketplace platform facilitating trade between small retailers and customers and provides logistics. The dataset contains real wholesale data with order details, order status, price, shipping, payment methods as well as customer location, product characteristics and customer reviews. Information about business partners as well as customer data have been anonymized.

METHODS:
This analysis examines company’s four main areas: revenue and financial trends over 18 months, number of customers and sellers, product categories and order processing details, customer satisfaction. Data preparation, analysis and visualisations were prepared mainly in PowerBI.

FINDINGS:
1. Company’s revenue rise is driven mainly by new customers as there is low customer retention rate.
2. There is a slight decrease in customer and order numbers in the 2nd half of 2018.
3. Customers rarely buy more than 1 item and some products are preferred more than others.
4. Positive customer reviews are prevalent in closer locations and correlate with shorter delivery times. Moreover, customer preferences for providing feedback are mostly bimodal and the number of negative reviews is not even across product categories.

CONCLUSIONS:
Olist Store could improve customer retention rate and encourage customers to buy more than 1 product to reduce shipping costs/product per client.
Expanding to more distant markets could be difficult without significant improvement of order delivery times.
Over-reliance on products more dominant in sales could be risky if market trends shift.
